\[
F = G\,\frac{m_1 m_2}{r^2}
\]

To compare the gravitational force exerted on a baby by Jupiter and by the baby’s mother when the baby is held close to the mother’s chest, consider the ratio of forces. The baby’s mass cancels:
\[
\frac{F_J}{F_M}
=
\frac{M_J / r_J^2}{M_M / r_M^2}
=
\frac{M_J}{M_M}\left(\frac{r_M}{r_J}\right)^2
\]

Assumed values:
\[
M_M = 65\,\mathrm{kg}
\qquad
m_b = 3.5\,\mathrm{kg}
\qquad
r_M = 0.20\,\mathrm{m}
\]
\[
M_J = 1.898\times 10^{27}\,\mathrm{kg}
\]

Earth--Jupiter distance:
\[
r_J = 6.28\times 10^{11}\,\mathrm{m}\quad(\text{closest})
\]
\[
r_J = 9.28\times 10^{11}\,\mathrm{m}\quad(\text{farthest})
\]

Mother--baby gravitational force:
\[
F_M = G\,\frac{(65)(3.5)}{(0.20)^2}
\approx 3.8\times 10^{-7}\,\mathrm{N}
\]

Jupiter--baby gravitational force:

Closest approach:
\[
F_J = G\,\frac{(1.898\times 10^{27})(3.5)}{(6.28\times 10^{11})^2}
\approx 1.1\times 10^{-6}\,\mathrm{N}
\]

Farthest separation:
\[
F_J \approx 5.2\times 10^{-7}\,\mathrm{N}
\]

Force comparison:
\[
\left.\frac{F_J}{F_M}\right|_{\text{closest}}
\approx
\frac{1.1\times 10^{-6}}{3.8\times 10^{-7}}
\approx 2.9
\]
\[
\left.\frac{F_J}{F_M}\right|_{\text{farthest}}
\approx
\frac{5.2\times 10^{-7}}{3.8\times 10^{-7}}
\approx 1.4
\]
